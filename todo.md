## Writing Tone

Use this as the default tone for future drafts in this series.

- Write in a direct, plainspoken first-person voice.
- Stay close to the actual work: the repo, the bug, the design choice, the release problem, the tradeoff.
- Prefer concrete details over atmosphere.
- Keep the opening tight. Get to the point quickly.
- Use commit messages and repo history as supporting evidence, not as a structure for the post.
- Avoid dramatic phrasing, grand conclusions, and philosophical detours unless they are genuinely earned.
- Avoid paragraphs that sound nice but do not add information.
- Keep bullet lists short and functional. Do not turn the post into a changelog.
- Explain what happened, why it mattered, what changed, and what was learned.
- When talking about AI, keep it practical. Describe where it helped, where it did not, and what still required judgment.
- Aim for something that sounds like notes from real engineering work, not an essay about engineering.
- Keep endings short. Do not zoom out into a grand conclusion if the useful point has already been made.
- Do not overstate the importance of a bug, feature, or refactor. Let the specifics carry the post.
